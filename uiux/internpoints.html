Introduction

In the summer of 2017 I was an intern mentor. The setup is two teams of 5 (a designer, two software engineers and two analysts) competing against each other to create the "most successful" website. Having been an intern myself, I was highly invested in the success of the program. Seeing a lot of changes we wanted to make from the previous year, the other mentors and I decided on a new tiered points system to measure the success of the site, in addition to milestones to be completed to help guide them as they first begin (example: create wireframe, create mockup, deploy site, along with some fun ones like drinking 200 cans of Polar seltzer). The more tiers and milestones they reached, the more points they got, while they lost a point every $200 spent on advertisements.

To keep track of these points, I designed this mockup and worked closely with the engineering mentors to develop an internal site to Cogo Labs that would track the intern's points. It was successfully used throughout the summer and will be reused again in the following years.
